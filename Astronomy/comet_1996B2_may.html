Comet Hyakutake (1996B2)

Two images from May 1996

These images were scanned using a Logitech PageScan Colour scanner at 200 DPI, from Kodak Tri-X pan 400 (push processed to 800) black-and-white prints. Simple image processing was then carried out (JPEG to GIF conversion, cropping, brightness adjustment) using PageScan Image Editor v1.2, Graphic Workshop v1.1n, and WinGIF v1.4.

The photos were taken from a tripod-mounted Pentax K1000 camera - hence the obvious trailing - by David Benn from a private property 20 minutes south-east of Launceston.

Distances below are given in Astronomical Units, where 1 AU = 149,597,870 km.

2 minute exposure, from 05:46:30 am to 05:48:30 am on 24/5/96 (135mm at f/2.8)
Distance from Earth: 1.16 AU; Distance from Sun: 0.71 AU

Hyakutake peeking through the clouds...
1 minute exposure, from 05:30 am to 05:31 am on 15/5/96 (50mm at f/3.5)
